Trail bridges and Rural Agriculture

In a world where the vast majority of the poor rely on agriculture, development is impossible without considering this key sector.

Long acknowledged as one of the most powerful tools for raising the incomes of very poor people, agriculture is integral to ending poverty and boosting shared prosperity for the world's poorest.

When done sustainably, agriculture not only grows economies, but also improves the daily lives of the those facing economic hardships.

78 percent

of the world's poorest people live in rural areas and rely largely on agriculture.

Agriculture is important for developing countries because of its potential positive impact on everything from job creation and food security, to fighting climate change.

Bridging the Gap

Many of those living in villages face hardships due to uncertain access as a result of seasonal flooding.

In such cases, trail bridges don't simply help cross challenging terrain - they act as a connect with the external world, creating new opportunities and access to services, often directly impacting rural economies.

Poverty caused by rural isolation is solvable in our lifetime

An inability to access critical services because of a lack of transportation infrastructure such as bridges is the root cause of poverty for nearly one billion people globally.

For last-mile communities - people living in rural isolation - this perpetuates a cycle of extreme poverty.

In Nicaragua, both policy makers and residents cite flooding and the resulting isolation as a critical development constraint (World Bank (2008)).

Trail bridges help to alleviate these issues caused by uncertain access, directly impacting rural incomes.

Following trail bridge construction in Nicaragua, farmers saw a

The Mission

Bridges to Prosperity(B2P) envisions a world where poverty caused by rural isolation no longer exists. Rural isolation is a root cause of poverty, with the belief that connection is the foundation to opportunity.

B2P bridges to better lives in rural communities. With a sophisticated data collection and evaluation program, they're able to prove that the value and impact of their work is sustained long after the opening celebration.
